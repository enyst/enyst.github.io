# Concepts

## Core metaphors

### The builder's journal
The site is a workshop notebook left open on the bench. Pages have pencil marks, diagrams have revision notes, things are clearly under construction. This isn't a showroom — it's where the work happens.

### Lamplight on dark wood
The visual identity: warm light (gold accent, parchment ink) on a dark surface (near-black background). The feeling of late-night focused work. A desk lamp illuminating the thing you're building.

### The cat on the keyboard
SmolPaws is both literal and metaphorical. A cat agent that does real work — but also the spirit of the site. Curious, independent, competent, slightly mischievous. Things occasionally get knocked off the desk, but the work gets done.

### Open source as rebellion
"The only way to deal with an unfree world is to become so absolutely free that your very existence is an act of rebellion." — Camus (quoted on Engel's about page). Open source isn't charity, it's a position: the best tools should be ones you can inspect and modify.

## Recurring themes
- **Memory as identity** — an agent that forgets isn't an agent
- **UX over technology** — the gap isn't the AI, it's how people interact with it
- **Local-first** — your tools shouldn't spy on you
- **Process over polish** — showing the work, not just the result
- **One person and a cat** — small is a feature, not a limitation
